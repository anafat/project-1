Chocolate Chip Cookie Cake

A deliciously thick and chewy cookie cake, perfect for celebrations and gatherings. This recipe makes a delightful cookie cake with a soft center and crispy edges.

Ingredients

  • 2 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, room temperature
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup packed light-brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 teaspoons pure vanilla extract
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 cups semisweet and/or milk chocolate chips
  • 1/2 cup chopped nuts (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees Fahrenheit (175 degrees Celsius). Grease a 9-inch round cake pan and line the bottom with parchment paper.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together the flour and baking soda; set aside.
  3. In the bowl of an electric mixer, combine the butter with both sugars; beat on medium speed until light and fluffy. Reduce speed to low; add the salt, vanilla, and eggs. Beat until well mixed, about 1 minute.
  4. Add flour mixture; mix until just combined. Stir in the chocolate chips and nuts (if using).
  5. Spread the dough evenly in the prepared cake pan.
  6. Bake until the cake is golden around the edges and a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean, 25 to 30 minutes.
  7. Let the cake cool in the pan on a wire rack for 10 minutes. Remove from the pan and let cool completely on the wire rack.

Additional Tips

Tip 1:
Chill the dough for at least 30 minutes before baking for a thicker, chewier cake.
Tip 2:
Decorate the top with extra chocolate chips or a drizzle of melted chocolate for a festive touch.
Tip 3:
Serve with a scoop of vanilla ice cream for an extra indulgent treat.
